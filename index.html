About

I am a generalist engineering student with a strong focus on materials, sustainability, and real-world constraints. What drives me most is working on concrete problems, where ideas must survive reality, not just theory. Beyond engineering, I am deeply passionate about alpine sports and the outdoors. Alpinism has shaped the way I think and work: preparation matters, resources are limited, conditions are never ideal, and adaptation is constant. In the mountains, poor decisions have immediate consequences — a mindset that naturally translates into engineering and project management. I am particularly interested in designing and improving products that are durable, efficient, and meaningful in real conditions. Combining my passion for sport with engineering is a long-term goal, and companies like Decathlon strongly resonate with my values: accessibility, performance, sustainability, and user-centered design. Whether on a technical project, in the field, or in extreme environments, I enjoy taking responsibility, working as part of a team, and delivering reliable solutions that make sense both technically and humanly.
